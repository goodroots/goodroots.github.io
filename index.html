Our Organic Farming Philosophy

Our philosophy is to work in harmony with all that nature provides. We focus on having rich and well-structured soil that provides all the necessary nutrients to the plants. The vegetables from the garden grow healthy and vibrant without the need for pesticides or herbicides. This also minimizes water use and keeps the land rich and fertile for future generations

Cold Weather Greens

January is the coldest and quietest month in the garden. But that doesn’t mean that nothing is happening. We see green all winter. Wintering Broccoli, open to the sky, absorbs the pure rainfall and produces bigger and tasty florets, while frost makes kale taste sweeter. Why? After temperatures drop below freezing, starches turn into sugars. So enjoy winter greens!

Happy

Gardening is not just a means of producing food but a symbol of the spiritual path. It gives us time to reflect on our lives and renew ourselves.  Being in nature helps us to find peace and happiness from within ourselves.

Healthy

We practice biodynamic French intensive gardening, a method of gardening with nature to foster healthy, vibrant plants in a small space and with less water. We do not use herbicides, pesticides, animal products or chemicals that are harmful to life and the environment.

Harmonious

Our garden couldn’t flourish without wildlife.  Hence, in keeping with the Buddhist principles of reverence for life, we ensure an awareness of care and consideration in the way we use resources and coexist in harmony with the native fauna.

Home

Our farm is situated within the peaceful environment of a Buddhist monastery surrounded by groves of Dora cedars and giant redwoods, and grassy meadows.  Home to monks, nuns, laymen, laywomen, families, and students.  A happy, healthy, harmonious home for everyone!
